Anything about you

Big Brother Watch reveals that the UK government is taking "faceprints" of millions of people for facial recognition. It seems that even we are the owner of our face, we can't totally control where our facial data go.

When Facial Recognition Technology (FRT) scan a face, it doesn't record it as a picture, rather, it preserves a template of a face, and link to political identities, such as gender, race, age, and class. I covered the real face with blue background, The template face floated on the screen, text flashes around it: It is someone “like” us, but is it really who we are?

Pressing the button "But who am I", just like wiping a mirror. An alert “I am vast and complex” bumped out, the real self without any embellish appears, which is much more vivid.
